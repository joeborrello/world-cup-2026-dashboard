Projection = a goals-per-match rate × the team's expected remaining matches. The rate is regressed toward a modest prior early on (so a hot opening game doesn't project a record-shattering tally) and sharpens as more matches are played. Because goals come in whole numbers, the projection is shown as a range — the 80% most-likely band of additional goals (a Poisson interval about that rate), not a fractional figure. It's a transparent estimate, not a per-player model — a striker on a team projected deep into the knockouts is credited more upside than one likely to bow out early.
